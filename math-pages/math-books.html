Book List

This is a list of books that I've read throughout my high school and undergraduate schooling.

Currently Reading

  • Abstract Algebra, 3rd Edition (Dummit, Foote) - currently a rereading from my undergraduate days, going through the exercises. I am developing a solutions manual, found here.
  • Algebraic Topology (Hatcher) - very difficult, but rewarding text so far.

Finished

  • Riemannian Geometry, 3rd Edition (Petersen) - probably the hardest book I've read so far. Extremely challenging, but covers Riemannian geometry and does a deeper dive into differential geometry, in general.
  • Topology, 2nd Edition (Munkres) - a good balance of explanation and rigor without falling into verbosity. Also contains some Algebraic Topology, but nothing extensive.
  • Probability and Random Processes, 4th Edition (Grimmett, Stirzaker) - dense introduction to abstract probability theories with some terseness, but an extremely well-written reference when needed. Exercises are pretty challenging.
  • Principles of Mathematical Analysis, 3rd Edition (Rudin) - an extremely rigorous and (rather) terse introduction to real analysis. It was extremely rewarding to work through, but I suffered many a headache doing even 2 or 3 problems for my homework.
  • Introduction to Probability, 2nd Edition (Bertsekas, Tsitsiklis) - a little on the denser side, but really good at providing a solid foundation for probability theory.
  • Linear Algebra Done Right, 3rd Edition (Axler) - fun book that doesn't deal with determinants until the very end. Oddly read at times when students don't have a decent understanding of proof yet, so have some discrete math background first.
  • Elements of Set Theory (Enderton) - maybe the best introduction to set theory I decided to read before diving into real analysis. This deserves a medal in something.
  • Calculus: Early Transcendentals, 8th Edition (Stewart) - a regularly comprehensive overview of calculus. I suggest against the early exercises in sections as they're rote calculations and not very insightful. Good for practice and review, though, and the end exercises are more entertaining.
